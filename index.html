15 / Open questions

Risks and open questions

The ones written into the union contract, like credentials and structural roles, are dependable. Site-specific stipends can shift with budgets, so the plan counts on the contract-backed ones and treats the rest as upside.

The pension vests at five years; before that, every dollar I contributed is refundable, and the credential is permanent. The downside of trying it is limited.

Strikes are rare and time-bound, and the union exists precisely to protect pay and conditions. It is short-term disruption, not a structural risk to the career.

After tenure, union protection makes this among the steadiest jobs available, even with the district's budget pressures.

The teaching schedule leaves nights and summers to build, and if a venture scales I can reassess. The floor does not trap the upside.

The honest risk. This plan keeps entrepreneurship alive on the side rather than closing the door, so it de-risks the bet instead of abandoning it.
